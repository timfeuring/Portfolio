Showreel

Digital accessibility is more important than ever, but it’s hard to understand. For my Bachelor Thesis in Communication Design at HAW Hamburg, I researched ways to make accessibility principles understandable easier. The outcome: a 100+ pages theoretical thesis and a hands-on interactive experience for learning about digital accessibility patterns.

The Problem

The internet is an essential part of our everyday life, yet many digital products remain inaccessible – due to avoidable barriers. A missed opportunity – for inclusivity as well as for businesses, as accessible design benefits a wider audience. In 2024, 96% of the top one million websites failed basic accessibility tests.

Guidance is already there: the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) provide detailed information on how accessibility can be ensured in digital services. But still, missing web accessibility remains a huge issue. Why is that?

Research & Insights

To understand the problems with implementing digital accessibility, I conducted a multi-layered research approach, examining WCAG guidelines, a market research, and real-world challenges faced by designers and developers.

I analyzed how WCAG are structured and where designers and developers struggle to translate them into practice.

A market analysis revealed a gap between abstract guidelines and hands-on support: existing guides are often either too shallow or too technical. Tools can support, but not ensure accessibility.

Through interviews and surveys with designers, developers and other stakeholders of product development processes, I gathered insights into real-world obstacles.
